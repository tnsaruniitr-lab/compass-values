# Compass — Enrichment Menu

> Sourced research, 2026-07-05 (5 parallel web-research sweeps + synthesis). Every item cites a real, free/licensable resource and is honesty-compatible — the tools structure the *user's own* reasoning; none score, type, or flatter the person. Deduplicated against ROADMAP-100X.md (couples/team compare, AI reflection, retest/drift loop, Supabase+email, cohort norms, O*NET links, share cards, telemetry are already planned; noted where an item *deepens* one).

## Thesis

The single highest-value move for THIS product is to make measurement honesty itself the content — to convert Compass's biggest structural liability (individual-level Schwartz scores are noisy; ~4 of 19 refined values are weakly reliable; change scores sit on .16–.55 alphas) into its most defensible, un-copyable moat. In a Barnum-saturated quiz market, a product that publicly shows the uncertainty, re-sorts values by context to expose truths flat quizzes hide, and grounds every per-value claim in Schwartz's own primary text is doing something no horoscope competitor can imitate without exposing itself. So the enrichment menu is organized best-first by how much each item COMPOUNDS the honesty-transparency moat rather than merely adding a readout. Three principles filter everything: (1) the user authors the meaning (self-affirmation writes, own re-sorts, own consistency ratings) — never the app asserting; (2) additions must deepen the already-shipped machinery (stated-vs-lived sort, opposing-tensions readout, confidence chips, career module, permalink) rather than bolt on new one-shot outputs; (3) prefer free/open-license/citable sources and treat every licensing wall as a hard watch-out. The two transformative-but-large plays are Values-in-Context re-sorting (the "your money-self and family-self rank differently" truth no one ships) and a first-class Values-Conflict coach on the user's single sharpest measured tension. The cheapest durable wins are the evidence-backed values-affirmation micro-write and folding Bull's-Eye/VLQ importance-vs-consistency logic into the already-shipped gap sort so it becomes a recurring alignment ritual instead of a one-shot. Note: the action layer (decision matrix, WOOP, 10-10-10/Odyssey, job-crafting, values-budget, conversation prompts) is already folded into ENRICHMENT-IDEAS.md and is NOT re-listed here — this menu is the content-depth, product-pattern, data, and novel-differentiator layers that sit alongside it.

## Highest value-per-effort (start here)

- Values-affirmation micro-write (small / high): a 3-minute 'write about a time your top-traded value guided a real choice', Cohen/Sherman self-affirmation protocol, stored in the permalink, cited inline INSTEAD of flattery. Near-pure copy+logic on top of the shipped result; the honest antidote to Barnum. https://www.geoffreylcohen.com/s/AF_Meta-Analysis-Affirmation-in-School.pdf
- 80th-Birthday elicitation (small / medium): a warm non-morbid ideal-self prompt whose user-authored phrases feed the existing 'crown' headline — evidence-based elicitation, fully user-generated. https://ubwp.buffalo.edu/maketodaymeaningful/wp-content/uploads/sites/139/2020/04/80th-Birthday-Exercise-for-Values-Exploration.pdf
- MI reflection scaffolds (small / medium): a vetted library of neutral, open, discrepancy-surfacing prompt templates ('You ranked X near the top; where in the last week did that show up, and where did something else win?') keyed per top/traded value — deepens the already-found conversation prompts and the roadmap's profile-grounded AI reflection by grounding tone in Motivational Interviewing evidence. https://motivationalinterviewing.org/personal-values-card-sort
- 'How we know / here's the uncertainty' science library (medium / high): first-class permalinkable pages that reuse RESEARCH_AND_PLAN.md's caveats section verbatim — the cheapest way to turn the group-vs-individual-validity liability into an SEO/AEO trust surface no Barnum competitor can copy. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1073191121998760

## Tier 1 — Honesty-as-content moat (compounds the core differentiator; these are the un-copyable plays)

### Values-in-Context re-sort: contrast the work-self vs money-self vs family-self hierarchy  _(transformative impact · large effort)_

**What:** After the baseline 10-value sort, offer an optional short re-sort of the SAME values primed by a specific life domain ('rank these as they guide you AT WORK', then '…WITH MONEY' or '…WITH FAMILY'). Show the two circumplex positions side-by-side and name where they diverge ('Security sits low in your general map but jumps to #2 in the money frame'). Frame honestly: this surfaces context-varying INSTANTIATION of stable underlying values, not a different 'self' per room. Each domain sort inherits Phase-1 confidence gating independently.

**Source:** Domain/role value variability (Sagiv & Schwartz-adjacent): https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6326475/ ; Constructivist context-of-construal critique (Diversity of Value Construal): https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10720537.2021.1965510

**Value:** The single most 'nobody else ships this' insight. Flat one-hierarchy quizzes structurally cannot show that your money-self and family-self prioritize differently — a concrete, falsifiable, often uncomfortable truth. Adds a WITHIN-person cross-domain axis that deepens (not duplicates) the roadmap's stated-vs-lived and opposing-tensions work, and it reuses only the user's own re-sorts (real data, no inference).

### Values-Conflict coach: make the user's sharpest measured tension a first-class navigable module  _(transformative impact · medium effort)_

**What:** Turn the already-shipped 'opposing-value tensions' readout from a static label into a short guided ACT-style module for the user's single most-opposed high-value pair (auto-seeded from the two most-opposed high values on the circumplex, e.g. Security ↔ Self-Direction). Steps: name the specific clash the data shows; validate it as a PERMANENT tension to navigate, not a defect to fix; elicit a real situation where it bit; end on a 'both-directions' framing ('a step toward Self-Direction that still honors Security') rather than picking a winner.

**Source:** ACT values-conflict clarification (Security/Conformity vs Self-Direction/Stimulation as canonical clash; conflicts are ongoing tensions to navigate): https://positivepsychology.com/values-clarification/ ; conflicting-values coaching framing: https://www.rhythmandresolve.com/blog1/2019/7/24/acceptance-and-commitment-therapy-lets-explore-values

**Value:** Users don't feel their 'top 3 values' as a pain point — they feel the CONFLICT (leave the stable job or chase the passion?). Coaching the exact tension the sort already surfaces is the highest-empathy moment Compass can offer, and it operates uniquely on Compass's own measured circumplex geometry. Deepens the shipped opposing-tensions feature rather than adding a new readout; never scores or prescribes a resolution.

### 'How we know / here's the uncertainty' — a browsable, cited science library as the trust moat  _(high impact · medium effort)_

**What:** A first-class, permalinkable section that shows the working: for each claim Compass makes, a plain-language 'what the evidence supports', 'what it can't', and 'why we hedge' — e.g. 'Schwartz's structure is validated at the GROUP level; short subscales are reliable for research, not individual diagnosis, so we show confidence chips and abstain.' Surface the actual reliability ranges (PVQ-RR subscale alphas ~.55–.81; ESS PVQ-21 tradition .18/.16, security .40/.39; ~4 of 19 refined values weakly measured) and the honest 'MDS/circumplex reproduction is the PRIMARY validity claim, not coefficient alpha.' Reuse RESEARCH_AND_PLAN.md §'Caveats, corrections & open problems' verbatim as source material.

**Source:** PVQ-RR psychometrics across 49 groups incl. subscale reliabilities + 'group research not individual diagnostics': https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1073191121998760 ; open-science transparency-as-credibility norms: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jopy.12626 ; internal source already written: /Users/arunsharma/Documents/New project/compass-values/RESEARCH_AND_PLAN.md §Caveats

**Value:** Converts Compass's biggest structural liability (individual-level imprecision) into its clearest differentiator and a natural SEO/AEO credibility surface (owner's own AEO expertise applied to the product). A public 'here's the uncertainty' library is a trust artifact no Barnum competitor can copy without exposing itself. Directly discharges roadmap risks #1 (group vs individual validity) and #2 (noisy change scores) by making them visible to users.

## Tier 2 — Deepen the shipped stated-vs-lived loop with validated, free instruments

### Bull's-Eye / VLQ importance-vs-consistency ritual: upgrade the one-shot gap sort into a recurring alignment practice  _(high impact · medium effort)_

**What:** Fold the ACT Bull's-Eye / Valued Living Questionnaire logic into the already-shipped stated-vs-lived module: importance is already known from the sort; separately ask how CONSISTENT actions were this week, plot the gap on a dartboard, and highlight the high-importance / low-consistency values as the honest 'work here first' zone. No streaks, no score, no badges — the metric is the user's OWN self-rated alignment, revisitable over time. Pick ONE gap instrument (Bull's-Eye OR VLQ), not both — they overlap conceptually.

**Source:** Bull's-Eye worksheet (free) + psychometric validation (Lundgren et al. 2012): https://thehappinesstrap.com/upimages/Long_Bull's_Eye_Worksheet.pdf and https://www.researchgate.net/publication/232239325_The_Bull's-Eye_Values_Survey_A_Psychometric_Evaluation ; VLQ (free, importance-minus-consistency, 10 domains): https://stevenchayes.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/The-Valued-Living-Questionnaire.pdf

**Value:** Gives the stated-vs-lived gap a repeatable HOME — a validated alignment practice, not a vanity metric — and a non-gamified retention loop that fits the honesty brand. DEEPENS the roadmap's reliable-change-gated retest loop honestly: importance is the stable trait, consistency is a within-week behavioral self-rating (not a noisy 2-item difference score), so it avoids 'charging users to watch measurement noise.' Both instruments are free ACT materials.

### Values-affirmation micro-write (the evidence-based antidote to Barnum flattery)  _(high impact · small effort)_

**What:** After the reveal, offer a 3-minute guided free-write: 'Pick your top-traded value and write about a time it actually guided a choice.' This is the exact Steele/Cohen/Sherman self-affirmation protocol (rank values, write on the top one and why it's meaningful), with Pennebaker's emotion→cause→insight structure for the prompt. Store the write in the URL permalink so it persists. Cite the science inline as the credibility source INSTEAD of flattering copy.

**Source:** Cohen & Sherman values-affirmation meta-analysis / protocol: https://www.geoffreylcohen.com/s/AF_Meta-Analysis-Affirmation-in-School.pdf ; Sherman 2013 review: https://labs.psych.ucsb.edu/sherman/david/sites/labs.psych.ucsb.edu.sherman.david/files/pubs/sherman_2013.pdf ; Pennebaker expressive-writing evidence: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10300201/ ; procedure summary: https://learning.northeastern.edu/the-power-of-self-affirmation/

**Value:** Depth and durability with zero Barnum: the insight is USER-authored and research-validated (225+ studies show it reduces defensiveness and broadens perspective), giving a reason to sit with the result rather than screenshot-and-leave. It is literally an evidence-based writing prompt — no percentages, no type, no prediction — the exact opposite of flattery. (Same intervention surfaced independently in Sweep A and Sweep B; merged here.)

### 80th-Birthday elicitation (gentler ideal-self prompt) feeding the user-authored 'crown' line  _(medium impact · small effort)_

**What:** User imagines their 80th-birthday celebration and writes the toast they'd most want a loved one to give — surfacing values as the life they'd want to have lived, with an explicit non-morbid framing (birthday toast, not funeral eulogy). Use the resulting user-authored phrases as raw material for Compass's user-authored 'crown' headline. Comes with a written rationale and facilitation notes.

**Source:** Buffalo/ACBS 80th-Birthday exercise (free, educational-use): https://ubwp.buffalo.edu/maketodaymeaningful/wp-content/uploads/sites/139/2020/04/80th-Birthday-Exercise-for-Values-Exploration.pdf and https://contextualscience.org/80th_birthday_exercise_values_exploration

**Value:** A warm, narrative on-ramp that produces the user's own value words in their own voice — perfect raw material for the user-authored 'crown' line, and gentler than mortality-based prompts. DEEPENS the existing user-authored crown by giving it an evidence-based elicitation that generates the raw phrases, fully user-generated (no Barnum).

## Tier 3 — Per-value depth layer (rigorous, citable spine — a natural honest paywall candidate)

### Per-value 'deep read' built on Schwartz's canonical central-goal statements + cited action-levers  _(high impact · medium effort)_

**What:** An on-demand DEEP READ for any single value on the user's circumplex, built as a four-part card: (1) Schwartz's own one-sentence central motivational goal (e.g. Power = 'social status and prestige, control or dominance over people and resources'; Universalism = 'understanding, appreciation, tolerance, and protection for the welfare of all people and for nature'); (2) how it shows up; (3) its built-in tension with its circumplex opposite; (4) 2–3 concrete 'levers' to honor it this week. Ground every card in Schwartz's primary text and its 4 higher-order conflicts, not blog paraphrase. Model the depth/action structure on CliftonStrengths' per-theme 'Ideas for Action' and Sparketype's premium depth — but keep content Schwartz-grounded.

**Source:** Schwartz (2012) 'An Overview of the Schwartz Theory of Basic Values', open access: https://scholarworks.gvsu.edu/orpc/vol2/iss1/11/ ; Theory of Basic Human Values (CC BY-SA 4.0): https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_Basic_Human_Values ; CliftonStrengths per-theme Ideas for Action (structure model): https://storecontent.gallup.com/pdf/report_samples/insight_action/strengths_insight_and_action_planning_guide_en-us.pdf ; Sparketype premium depth model: https://sparketype.com/

**Value:** A 'go deeper on THIS value' path that rewards curiosity and is a natural, honest paywall candidate later (depth people actually pay for), grounded in Schwartz's published definitions rather than invented traits. Composes three sweep findings (Sweep A goal-statements spine + Sweep B premium deep-read + Sweep D 'in work terms') into one layered module. Deepens the roadmap's O*NET/career work by adding a values-FIRST depth layer.

### O*NET Work Values crosswalk: 'your values, in work terms' (CC BY 4.0)  _(high impact · medium effort)_

**What:** The O*NET database ships a 6-category Work Values taxonomy (Achievement/Independence/Recognition/Relationships/Support/Working Conditions) plus ~900 occupations scored on those values. The six map coherently onto Schwartz higher-order dimensions (Achievement/Recognition→Self-Enhancement, Independence→Openness/Self-Direction, Relationships/Support→Self-Transcendence, Working Conditions→Conservation). Surface as a 'your values, in work terms' translation layer AND — same DB, one download — the bridge to the already-planned O*NET occupation-family links. Display CC BY attribution to US DOL/ETA and label the Schwartz↔O*NET map as a documented THEORETICAL correspondence, not identity.

**Source:** O*NET Work Values explorer: https://www.onetonline.org/explore/workvalues/ ; database + CC BY 4.0 license: https://www.onetcenter.org/database.html and https://www.onetcenter.org/license_db.html

**Value:** Turns abstract circumplex positions into concrete, occupation-linked work language ('you'd value autonomy over recognition') from a free government dataset — no self-invented mapping for the career side. DEEPENS the roadmap's planned O*NET career-family link by adding the values-side crosswalk, not just the RIASEC/interest side, and one download serves both.

## Tier 4 — Retention & artifact patterns (honest analogs of what the best reflection apps do)

### 'A map for someone you love' — empathic-accuracy gifting mechanic (NOT compatibility scoring)  _(high impact · medium effort)_

**What:** A gifting/relational mode where person A optionally GUESSES how person B would rank their values BEFORE B takes it, then the reveal shows the gap between 'how well I thought I knew you' and B's actual self-sort — framed as a conversation starter about the surprises, never a match/compatibility number. Ships as a 'give this map to someone you love' share flow: the gift is the invitation + the guess; the payload is curiosity about the divergences.

**Source:** Empathic accuracy (inferring a close other's priorities is measurable, improves with transparency): https://bpspsychub.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/bjso.12612 ; couples benefit from talking about values, divergences as the productive start: https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/happiness-is-a-state-of-mind/202108/why-its-so-important-for-couples-to-talk-about-their

**Value:** An emotionally sticky, shareable relational feature that CANNOT be read as 'do we match?' — it measures how well you know someone and rewards curiosity about differences. Distinct from the roadmap's couples-compare (which compares two FINISHED results): the novel act here is the GUESS, creating a real 'oh, I didn't realize that mattered to you' moment. Strong viral/gifting loop that stays inside the no-compatibility firewall.

### 'Your values this week' appointment artifact fed by 5-second value-moment tags (not journaling)  _(medium impact · medium effort)_

**What:** A weekly recap card (Daylio/Rosebud weekly-insights pattern) that surfaces which of the user's OWN top values showed up in the tiny moments they logged that week, plus one recurring tension. Feed it NOT with daily journaling but with a How-We-Feel-style 5-second tap: in a real moment, the user names WHICH of their top values is at stake or in tension right now (selecting from THEIR OWN mapped values). Honest gate: only report what was actually logged, show n, and ABSTAIN when sparse ('only 2 moments logged — not enough to summarize'), mirroring Compass's existing honest-abstain.

**Source:** Daylio weekly report + correlations: https://daylio.net/ ; Rosebud weekly growth insights: https://www.rosebud.app/ ; How We Feel granularity (Yale/Marc Brackett Mood Meter): https://marcbrackett.com/how-we-feel-app-3/

**Value:** A recurring reason to return that feels like a gift, not a chore, and teaches a personal values-vocabulary the way How We Feel teaches emotion-vocabulary — turning abstract circumplex knowledge into a live noticing skill at near-zero effort per entry. The tag is also the low-effort data source that powers the weekly recap and feeds the stated-vs-lived gap. Note the honesty overlap with Sweep E's ESM-lite prompt: if built, ship the 'this samples constrained behavior/appraisal, not your values directly' caveat in the UI.

### Single daily 'values note' artifact + compassionate no-punishment return mechanics  _(medium impact · medium effort)_

**What:** Build Co-Star's one-beautiful-artifact-per-day discipline (never notification spam), but honest: one short daily prompt drawn from the user's OWN traded-off values — 'You ranked Benevolence above Achievement. Where will that show up today?' — as a stark, screenshot-worthy card. Pair with Finch-style return mechanics: track returns WITHOUT loss-aversion streaks ('welcome back — here's what's shifted in how you've been noticing your values', never 'you broke a 12-day streak'). Explicitly reject Co-Star/Duolingo guilt patterns.

**Source:** Co-Star one-notification/day + Day-at-a-Glance craft: https://medium.com/demagsign/how-the-design-of-the-astrology-app-co-star-is-conquering-the-masses-d6b6d235c806 ; Stoic morning/evening rhythm: https://www.getstoic.com/ ; Finch no-punitive-streak retention teardown: https://www.deconstructoroffun.com/blog/x0hd2ssr80y5n7gv0w967pg7hwd7tl

**Value:** A delightful, capped-at-once/day artifact and a genuine reason to return that never becomes fatigue, plus organic shareability — honest because the prompt is built from the user's REAL ranked values (it asks a question rather than predicting fate). Finch's compassion-over-manipulation stance matches honesty-first; this keeps Compass a wellbeing tool, not a slot machine. Complements (doesn't duplicate) the roadmap's one-shot share cards by making a recurring artifact.

## Tier 5 — Warmth & context sources (free, provenance-gated; small delight without Barnum)

**What:** The European Social Survey administers the 21-item Schwartz PVQ to 500k+ respondents across 80+ countries. Its published 'Findings from the Human Values Scale' report and papers give summary distributions and demographic patterns (Universalism/Benevolence rising over time; documented age/gender differences). **Value:** The ONLY defensible way to say 'relative to others' before Compass has its own cohort norms — grounded in the canonical Schwartz dataset. DEEPENS the roadmap's Phase-4 cohort/percentile-norms item by giving an interim, external, citable baseline instead of waiting for own-data scale. Read the watch-out: this is the licensing-sensitive one.

**What:** One tasteful, source-attributed quotation per Schwartz value from Wikiquote (which uniquely maintains 'Sourced / Disputed / Misattributed' sections), cross-checked against Quote Investigator and hard-excluding anything unsourced/misattributed. **Value:** Adds warmth/reflection WITHOUT Barnum flattery — because each line is real, attributed, and value-relevant rather than generic inspiration, and the provenance gate makes it defensible where every cheap quote API is a fake-attribution minefield. Open Library/Gutendex give a no-cost, no-lock-in 'read more about what you value' shelf a solo dev can ship and even monetize later. All curation is editorial — label it 'suggested', don't imply an algorithmic method.

## Watch-outs — do NOT add (honesty or licensing walls)

- VIA Character Strengths is NOT freely embeddable. The survey is free to TAKE but the VIA-IS items and scoring are copyrighted (paid license via VIA Pro / research program); re-implementing the questionnaire inside Compass breaks both IP and validity. Do a one-way HAND-OFF ('want a complementary strengths lens? take the free VIA survey →'), never an integration. If you conceptually relate values→strength families, label it as your interpretation, not VIA-endorsed scoring. https://www.viacharacter.org/researchers/assessments/via-is
- ESS micro-data is licensed NON-COMMERCIAL only. If Compass ever monetizes, use ONLY the PUBLISHED aggregate figures from the reports/papers (facts/statistics aren't copyrightable), never the redistributed dataset. Second caveat: ESS is Europe-skewed and uses PVQ-21, so any comparison to a non-European user or a differently-worded Compass sort must be flagged as approximate — 'relative to others' here is an interim, caveated baseline, not a percentile verdict.
- The ESM-lite / value-moment tag measures CONSTRAINED behavior and momentary appraisal, NOT values directly — ship that disclosure baked into the UI (it is on-brand as a moat, not a disclaimer). Keep it optional and local-first to match the no-backend privacy posture; do NOT auto-infer values from behavior — hand the contrast to the user to interpret. Same rule for the weekly recap: report only what was logged, show n, and abstain when sparse rather than fabricate a narrative.
- Do NOT reproduce copyrighted verbatim content: Gottman Card Decks / Aron 36 Questions (link out to the free apps — already noted in the action-layer sweep), and do NOT verbatim-embed The Good Project Value Sort cards or the paid Michigan Job Crafting booklet assets — build the free published methods and attribute. For Wikiquote, CC BY-SA requires attribution + share-alike AND surfacing ONLY 'Sourced' quotes; the whole point vs cheap quote APIs is NOT propagating misattributions.
- The Miller/Rollnick card sort's 83 folk-values are NOT the 10 Schwartz constructs — use it as a definition/label bank only, never as the taxonomy, or you re-introduce a competing (unvalidated) value model under the Schwartz backbone. Same discipline for O*NET: label the Schwartz↔O*NET-work-values mapping a documented THEORETICAL correspondence, not identity, and present O*NET Work Styles (if used at all) as trait CONTINUA/ranges, never a computed 'style score' derived from the values sort.
- Pick ONE gap instrument (Bull's-Eye OR VLQ), not both — they overlap conceptually and shipping two invites the 'flat self-rating' fatigue the honesty brand exists to avoid. And keep the recurring rituals (Bull's-Eye consistency, daily note, weekly recap) strictly no-score / no-streak-loss / no-badge: any slide back toward a graded number, a named 'type', or loss-aversion streaks re-introduces exactly the Barnum + dark-pattern artifacts Phase 1 killed.

## Appendix — Action-layer tools (the "so what do I do?" cluster)

*From the first research pass; folded into the tiers above but listed here with full sourcing.*

### Values-Weighted Decision tool (reuse Compass's own value weights as decision criteria)  _(medium effort)_

**What:** A small 'Bring a real choice' tool: user names 2-4 options (e.g. Job A vs Job B vs stay). Compass pre-loads the user's TOP values from their existing circumplex result AS the pre-weighted criteria (weights = their measured value priorities, not invented). User scores each option 1-5 on how well it serves each value; the tool multiplies score x the user's value-weight and surfaces where options diverge — and explicitly separates 'factual disagreement' (you scored the same value differently) from 'values disagreement'. Output is a structured trade-off view, NOT a recommendation.

**Source:** Weighted decision matrix method, incl. explicit values-based use for 'buying homes, choosing universities, selecting career paths' and the factual-vs-values disagreement split — https://airfocus.com/blog/weighted-decision-matrix-prioritization/ and https://lucid.co/blog/weighted-decision-matrix

**Value:** This is THE bridge from 'here are your values' to 'so what do I do about this offer/move/relationship'. It's the highest-felt-value moment because it operates on a real pending decision, and it uniquely reuses Compass's already-measured weights — no other values app can auto-populate the criteria with the user's real value hierarchy.

### WOOP if-then planner (turn a values-gap into one concrete next action)  _(small effort)_

**What:** A 4-step guided card at the end of a result or gap-sort: Wish (a change the user wants, seeded from their biggest stated-vs-lived gap) -> Outcome (imagine best result) -> Obstacle (name the real inner obstacle) -> Plan (write one if-then implementation intention: 'If [obstacle], then I will [action]'). Store the if-then plan in the URL permalink so it persists.

**Source:** WOOP (Wish-Outcome-Obstacle-Plan), Gabriele Oettingen, evidence-based; official free templates + method at https://woopmylife.org/ and https://woopmylife.org/en/practice

**Value:** Converts insight into a single doable step — the difference between 'interesting quiz' and 'I did something'. Directly complements the stated-vs-lived gap: the gap tells you WHERE you're off, WOOP gives you the mechanism to close it. Retention hook without gamification gimmicks.

### 10-10-10 + Odyssey-style reframe for a charged decision  _(small effort)_

**What:** A reflection card that takes the user's pending choice and asks the 10-10-10 questions (consequences in 10 minutes / 10 months / 10 years), with the 10-year prompt explicitly anchored to the user's top measured values ('Does this honor [Self-Direction] in 10 years?'). Pair with a lightweight 'Two Roads' reframe (a stripped-down Odyssey Plan): sketch Life A (current path) and Life B (values-forward alternative) in a few lines each.

**Source:** Suzy Welch 10-10-10 — https://www.oprah.com/spirit/suzy-welchs-rule-of-10-10-10-decision-making-guide ; Designing Your Life Odyssey Plans, Burnett & Evans, official free worksheet PDF — https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Odysseys-Worksheet-1.pdf

**Value:** 10-10-10 is a 60-second intervention against present-bias for exactly the emotionally-charged choices (a conversation you're avoiding, a job you're scared to leave) where values are hardest to act on. The Odyssey reframe shows values aren't destiny-locked — there are multiple lives that honor them. Both feed directly off the values map.

### Job Crafting mini-tool (for the majority who won't change careers)  _(medium effort)_

**What:** A 'You don't have to quit' module: user lists their current task-blocks, tags each by energy (drains/neutral/energizes), then the tool cross-references against their top values to highlight tasks that clash with a core value and prompts small craftable moves (add a task that serves value X, reframe a draining task's meaning, shift a relationship). This is the free published method (before-sketch -> after-diagram, task/energy blocks), NOT a copy of the paid booklet.

**Source:** Wrzesniewski & Dutton job crafting; free full method in Berg, Dutton & Wrzesniewski (2013) 'Job Crafting and Meaningful Work' PDF — https://justinmberg.com/wp-content/uploads/Berg-Dutton-Wrzesniewski_2013.pdf ; official (paid) exercise home for attribution — https://positiveorgs.bus.umich.edu/cpo-tools/job-crafting-exercise/

**Value:** The career-archetype module implicitly suggests changing jobs; most users can't/won't. Job crafting serves the silent majority — 'make your CURRENT job fit your values better' — which is arguably more actionable and honest than career switching. It also deepens the career-archetype feature with a same-job path.

### Values-Budget / Calendar reflection (evidence for the stated-vs-lived gap)  _(medium effort)_

**What:** A reflection tool that asks the user to allocate their last month's spending — and separately a typical week's hours — across their top Compass values (or 'serves no value'). It then visually contrasts stated value priority vs actual money/time allocation, producing a concrete 'here's where your calendar and bank statement disagree with what you said matters' view. No account linking needed for v1 — manual buckets keep it private and honesty-safe.

**Source:** Values-based budgeting / spending audit method — https://www.truist.com/money-mindset/taking-charge-of-your-money/budgeting-by-values and https://m1.com/knowledge-bank/creating-a-value-based-budget-aligning-your-spending-with-personal-priorities/

**Value:** This is the sharpest possible deepening of the EXISTING stated-vs-lived gap sort: instead of self-report abstractions, it grounds the gap in the two least-lying signals a person has — where their money and time actually go. 'You ranked Benevolence #1 but 2% of your discretionary hours went there' is a genuine, felt gut-check.

### Values-framed reflective conversation prompts (link-out, not compatibility scoring)  _(small effort)_

**What:** For the (already-planned) couples/team compare mode, add a set of VALUES-GROUNDED open-ended conversation prompts generated from the pair's shared and divergent values (e.g. 'You both rank Security high but differ on Stimulation — when has that tension shown up for you?'). Frame strictly as reflection starters. For canonical decks, link OUT to the free Gottman Card Decks app and reference the Aron 36 Questions rather than reproducing copyrighted text.

**Source:** Gottman Card Decks (free app, Open-Ended Questions / Love Maps decks) — https://www.gottman.com/couples/apps/ ; Aron et al. 'The Experimental Generation of Interpersonal Closeness' (36 Questions), NYT popularization — https://www.nytimes.com/2015/01/09/style/no-37-big-wedding-or-small.html

**Value:** Gives the compare mode a 'now talk about it' payload — the action layer that makes a shared-values screen useful instead of just interesting. Turns divergences into curiosity-driven conversation rather than a scoreboard.
